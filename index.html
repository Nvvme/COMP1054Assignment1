"I Don't Want To Set The World On Fire" By The Ink Spots

This classic hit by The Ink Spots has been a favorite since its release in 1941. Its catchy melody and heartfelt lyrics resonate deeply, making it a timeless piece.

According to Bill Kenny Jr., son of lead singer Bill Kenny Sr., "It was just a love song – nothing political." However, during WWII, audiences associated the title line with atomic bombs.

I just want to start a flame in your heart
In my heart, I have but one desire
And that one wish is you, no other will do
